Building a Symptom-Based Disease Diagnosis Web App with Flask and Machine Learning

**Published:** December 6, 2025 | **Category:** AI in Healthcare

In the age of technology and information, access to accurate and timely healthcare is more critical than ever. With the increasing importance of remote healthcare solutions, we embarked on a journey to develop a **symptom-based disease diagnosis web application**. Leveraging **Flask** for the backend and a robust **Decision Tree Classifier model**, we created a user-friendly platform that can help users identify potential illnesses based on their reported symptoms.

üí° The Problem Statement

The project began with recognizing a common issue: people often experience symptoms and want quick answers about their health concerns. It can be challenging to differentiate between various diseases, especially when symptoms overlap. Our goal was to provide a convenient, pre-consultation solution for users to input their symptoms and receive potential initial diagnoses. **(Note: This app is for informational purposes only and is not a substitute for professional medical advice.)**

üõ†Ô∏è The Machine Learning Solution

We developed a web app that allows users to enter a list of symptoms they are experiencing. The app then uses a **pre-trained Decision Tree Classifier model** to predict the most likely disease based on the provided symptoms. Here's a breakdown of the workflow:

  1. Symptom Input: Users enter their symptoms through a user-friendly interface (e.g., dropdowns, checkboxes). The web app supports a wide range of symptoms, making it versatile for different scenarios.
  2. Data Processing: The input symptoms are converted into the numerical format (vector) required by the Machine Learning model.
  3. Machine Learning Model: We trained a **Decision Tree Classifier** using a comprehensive dataset linking symptoms to diseases. This model is ideal for hierarchical diagnosis and offers high interpretability. [Image of Decision Tree Algorithm flow chart]
  4. Prediction: The Flask backend utilizes the model to predict the most likely disease, providing users with instant information about potential health concerns.
  5. Additional Information: To enhance user experience, the app also provides additional information about the predicted disease. This includes a disease description, recommended **precautions**, suggested **medications**, **dietary advice**, and **workout tips**.

  • **Comprehensive Information:**

    Beyond just the diagnosis, the app offers complete descriptions, precautions, diet, and exercise guidance for the predicted illness.

  • **User-Centric Interface:**

    The web app boasts an intuitive and easy-to-navigate interface, ensuring a hassle-free experience for all users.
